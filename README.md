## Project Overview

This project was built as part of a data analyst assignment for **AtliQ Media**, a media company producing a one-hour TV show on the 2026 Tamil Nadu Assembly Election results. Unlike typical election coverage driven by political commentary and debates, AtliQ wanted something different — a **clean, fact-based show grounded entirely in official Election Commission of India (ECI) data**.

As the data analyst on this project, I was tasked with:
- Identifying the most compelling data stories from the 2026 results
- Supporting each story with clear, evidence-based charts
- Pitching insights in a format that helps the show's producers plan their segments

> ⚠️ **Scope boundary:** This analysis does not explain *why* any party won or lost nor does it make any predictions. It focuses solely on what the data shows.

---

## Problem Statement

AtliQ Media needs a structured, visual narrative of the 2026 Tamil Nadu election results to anchor their one-hour TV special. The show must be free of political opinion — every claim on screen must be traceable to official data. The challenge is to extract the most interesting and TV-worthy stories from raw ECI data and present them clearly enough for a general audience.

## Data Source

**Election Commission of India (ECI)**

All datasets, metrics and election-related information in this project have been sourced from official ECI records.

- Seat-wise and party-wise results for 2021 and 2026 Tamil Nadu Assembly Elections

## The Geographic Story — *"Where was political power concentrated?"*

Tamil Nadu's 234 constituencies were grouped into 6 regions for analysis:

| Region | Seats |
|---|---|
| Chennai Metro | 58 |
| North | 33 |
| Central | 41 |
| Kongu | 32 |
| Delta | 37 |
| South | 33 |

**Alliance-level findings:**
- In 2021, **DMK Alliance dominated 5 out of 6 regions**, with AIADMK Alliance retaining a stronghold in the Kongu Belt.
- By 2026, the political map became significantly **more fragmented** — TVK emerged as a major force, introducing genuine three-way regional competition.

**Party-level findings:**
- In 2026, **TVK became the dominant party** in Chennai Metro, North, Kongu and South regions.
- **DMK secured the majority** of constituencies in the Central region.
- **AIADMK led** in the Delta region.

---

## The Flip Story — *"How did political control change?"*

Between 2021 and 2026, **163 out of 234 constituencies changed their winning party** — a flip rate of approximately **70.1%**, the highest among recent Tamil Nadu Assembly Elections.

Key observations from the retention and flip analysis:
- **DMK** was able to retain **40 constituencies** from 2021; the rest flipped to other parties.
- **AIADMK** retained approximately **33% of its 2021 constituencies**.
- Nearly all major parties experienced significant seat losses between the two elections, indicating a major electoral reshaping rather than a gradual shift.

## The Turnout Story — *"How actively did people participate?"*

$$\text{Turnout \%} = \frac{\text{People Who Voted}}{\text{Total Eligible Voters}} \times 100$$

| Year | Turnout |
|---|---|
| 2021 | 73.6% |
| 2026 | **85.1%** |

Tamil Nadu recorded its **highest-ever voter turnout of 85.1% in 2026**, an increase of 11.5 percentage points over 2021 — reflecting unprecedented electoral engagement.

**Top 20 constituencies with the largest turnout increase** revealed two clear patterns:

- **Region Pattern:** Turnout increases were not uniform — certain regions saw disproportionately higher surges, pointing to localized electoral competition and political mobilization.
- **Reservation Category Pattern:** Turnout changes also varied by constituency reservation category (General, SC, ST), indicating different levels of participation across demographic segments.

> These constituencies showed signs of stronger electoral competition and higher political engagement — making them key focal points for AtliQ's show.

---

## Key Insights

1. **TVK's Rise:** TVK secured **108 constituencies**, emerging as the party with the highest seat count in the 2026 election — a new entrant reshaping the state's political landscape.

2. **Historic Flip Rate:** With **163 out of 234 constituencies flipping** between 2021 and 2026, the 70.1% flip rate is the highest in recent Tamil Nadu Assembly election history.

3. **Record Voter Turnout:** Tamil Nadu's **85.1% turnout** in 2026 is the state's highest ever, indicating a significant and widespread increase in electoral participation.

4. **Geographic Reshaping:** The electoral map was substantially redrawn. Multiple parties led in different regions, replacing the near-uniform dominance seen in 2021 — signaling an evolving and competitive political geography.
